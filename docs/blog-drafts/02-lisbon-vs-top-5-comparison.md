# Is Lisbon Europe's best vegan city? Comparing the top 5 with real data

Ask the average plant-based traveller for Europe's best vegan city and
you'll hear "Berlin" — followed, increasingly often, by "Lisbon".
Recent guidebooks have started treating Lisbon as a top-tier vegan
destination, but the rankings I've seen are usually opinion-based.

I run a vegan directory that holds 50,000+ places across 163
countries. Here's what happens when you compare Lisbon to its rivals
on actual data — venue counts, fully-vegan options, prices,
neighbourhood density.

## The five we compared

These are the five cities that consistently appear in every "best
European vegan city" list I've checked:

- **Berlin** — the long-standing benchmark
- **Lisbon** — the new contender most travel writers are excited about
- **Barcelona** — Mediterranean heavyweight, dense scene
- **Madrid** — Barcelona's larger but less-vegan-famous neighbour
- **Athens** — the under-rated entry on this list

All five have over 100 vegan or vegan-friendly venues. All five have
multiple dedicated 100% vegan restaurants. All five are accessible to
travellers — you can get from the airport to your first vegan meal
in under an hour at each.

## How we did this

Every "100% vegan verified" count in this post comes from a manual
check: menu PDF or web page on the venue's site, plus a recent
review pass. Anywhere we couldn't get to a confident "verified",
we left it tagged "mostly vegan" and excluded it from the
fully-vegan count. That's the bar.
