# Germany's tier-2 vegan cities, compared with real data

Berlin gets all the vegan press. Some of it is deserved — the city has
been a plant-based destination for over a decade. But Berlin is not the
only good vegan city in Germany, and for a traveller it might not even
be the best. After auditing every venue we have data on across four
tier-2 cities, here's the honest picture.

## What "tier-2" means here

We're comparing four cities that consistently come up in travel
guides but live in Berlin's shadow:

- **Düsseldorf** — Rhine-Ruhr business hub with a surprising plant-based density
- **Cologne** — younger student population, more counter-cultural vegan scene
- **Stuttgart** — Baden-Württemberg's wealthy regional capital, often overlooked
- **Hamburg** — Northern port city, distinctive food culture, harbour focus

Each has more than 50 vegan or vegan-friendly venues on PlantsPack —
enough to actually plan a trip around. None has Berlin's volume, but
volume isn't the only thing a traveller cares about.

## How we verified this

Every restaurant marked "100% vegan" was checked against:
1. The venue's current menu (PDF or web)
2. The venue's about/concept page
3. Recent reviews flagging any animal-product items

Anywhere we couldn't verify, we left the vegan_level at
"mostly_vegan" or "vegan_friendly" — and excluded those from the
"fully vegan" counts in this post. That's why our numbers are
smaller than other directories.
