# 24 accidentally vegan staples of Mediterranean cuisine

The most reliable way to eat well as a vegan in the Mediterranean is
not to ask for a vegan menu. It's to order dishes that have always
been vegan, in restaurants that have always served them. The Catholic
+ Orthodox + Muslim fasting calendars overlap in surprising ways, and
the region has thousands of years of plant-based food culture
embedded in everyday menus.

Here are 24 dishes — across Italy, Spain, Greece, Portugal, and Turkey
— that are vegan by default. Order them by name, in any decent
restaurant, and you don't need a substitution conversation.

## Italy (5 dishes)

### 1. Pizza marinara (Naples)

Tomato, garlic, oregano, olive oil. That's it — no mozzarella, no
anchovies. The original Neapolitan pizza, pre-dating margherita by a
century, is fully vegan. Order it specifically as "marinara" (not
"margherita") and confirm "senza acciughe" (without anchovies) — some
restaurants outside Naples have started adding them.

### 2. Pasta aglio e olio (Italy-wide)

Spaghetti, garlic, olive oil, chilli, parsley. A classic Roman pasta
that needs nothing else. If the restaurant tries to add parmesan,
say "senza formaggio".

### 3. Caponata (Sicily)

Sweet-and-sour aubergine stew with celery, olives, capers, tomato,
pine nuts. Naturally vegan, served as both side and antipasto.

### 4. Panelle (Palermo street food)

Chickpea flour fritters, fried, served in bread. Vegan, ancient, and
the best street food in Palermo for under €3.

### 5. Bruschetta al pomodoro

Toasted bread, olive oil, tomato, basil. Vegan by default at any
restaurant. "Bruschetta" alone can come with various toppings — ask
for "al pomodoro" specifically.

## Spain (5 dishes)

### 6. Gazpacho (Andalusia)

Cold soup of tomato, cucumber, pepper, onion, garlic, olive oil,
sherry vinegar, bread. Vegan and ubiquitous in summer.

### 7. Pan con tomate (Catalonia)

Bread rubbed with tomato, salt, olive oil. Sometimes garlic too.
Always vegan unless the restaurant is unusually creative.

### 8. Patatas bravas (Spain-wide)

Fried potatoes with brava sauce (smoked paprika + tomato + olive
oil + chilli). Watch out: the "aioli" version is not vegan. Order
just "patatas bravas" and confirm "sin alioli".

### 9. Espinacas con garbanzos (Sevilla)

Spinach with chickpeas, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika. A
naturally-vegan tapa with Moorish roots.

### 10. Pisto manchego (La Mancha)

Stewed tomatoes, courgette, onion, pepper. Vegan as long as you
skip the optional fried egg on top.

## Greece (5 dishes)

### 11. Fava (Santorini + nationwide)

Yellow split-pea purée with olive oil, lemon, capers, red onion.
The Santorini version uses local lava-soil-grown fava and is one
of the most distinctive vegan dishes on the planet.

### 12. Gemista (rural Greek tavernas)

Tomatoes and peppers stuffed with rice, herbs, sometimes pine
nuts. Often served as a "nistisima" (Lent) dish, so it's vegan.

### 13. Horta (Greece-wide)

Wild boiled greens dressed with olive oil and lemon. Every
taverna has them. Vegan, cheap, and the most underrated dish on
Greek menus.

### 14. Gigantes plaki

Giant white beans baked with tomato, onion, herbs. A staple of
Lenten cooking.

### 15. Dolmades (rice-stuffed)

Vine leaves stuffed with rice + herbs. The meat-stuffed version
exists; ask for the rice version specifically, which is the more
common one and vegan.

## Portugal (4 dishes)

### 16. Pão com tomate

Same as Spanish pan con tomate, slightly different ratio. Vegan
default.

### 17. Sopa de couve / caldo verde (often)

Cabbage and potato soup. The traditional version has chouriço
(sausage) — order it specifically "sem chouriço" and confirm. Many
modern Lisbon restaurants serve it vegan by default now.

### 18. Migas (Alentejo)

Bread, garlic, olive oil, fresh herbs. Traditional version
includes meat — ask for "migas vegetarianas" or "migas com
espargos" (asparagus) which is the vegan staple.

### 19. Tremoços

Lupin beans served in brine as a snack. Vegan, ancient, and
addictive. Every traditional café has them.

## Turkey (5 dishes)

### 20. Imam bayıldı

Aubergine stuffed with onion, garlic, tomato. The name literally
means "the imam fainted" — supposedly from how good it is. Vegan.

### 21. Mercimek çorbası (lentil soup)

Red lentil soup with cumin, sometimes mint butter on top. Order
"yağsız" if you want it strictly vegan (without butter), but most
restaurants serve it vegan by default.

### 22. Dolma (rice-stuffed)

Same family as Greek dolmades. Rice-stuffed version is vegan;
meat version isn't.

### 23. Tarhana

Fermented yogurt-and-grain soup. Vegan if made without yogurt
(some traditional versions are; ask). Mostly skip in Turkey but
worth knowing for Anatolian travel.

### 24. Simit + zeytin + domates (Turkish vegan breakfast)

Sesame ring bread + olives + tomatoes. The default street
breakfast everywhere in Turkey. Pair with hummus and you're set.

## How to use this list

Take a screenshot or print this list and check it when you're at
a restaurant without a vegan menu. Most of these dishes are on every
traditional menu in their region. You don't need to ask if the place
is "vegan-friendly" — you ask for the dish by name.
