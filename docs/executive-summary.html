What it is, honestly

PlantsPack is a map and feed centred on vegan venues. Roughly 11,000 venues are tagged fully vegan after a manual check (we look for non-vegan items on the menu and run a verification search before applying the label). Another ~37,000 are tagged vegan-friendly - they offer vegan options but also serve animal products. The rest carry source-inherited labels (vegan_options, mostly_vegan) we have not independently verified yet. We do not pretend every listing is checked.

Users browse cities, save trips, follow places, and post reviews. The platform is early-stage: tens of users today, daily development, no paid acquisition.

Why it exists

Existing directories mix vegan and non-vegan results in the same map pin and hide animal products in "vegetarian-friendly" tags. PlantsPack separates the two: fully-vegan venues carry a distinct verified badge, vegan-friendly venues are clearly labelled as such, and the filter UI lets users restrict to fully vegan with one tap.

What we believe makes the audience valuable

These are hypotheses based on the niche, not proven with our own data yet.

  • High intent (assumed). Users who reach a vegan-only directory typically arrive looking for somewhere to eat or stay.
  • Loyal niche. Vegan communities tend to share recommendations actively - we have seen this anecdotally in Reddit threads, not yet measured on platform.
  • Underserved by ads. Most food/travel marketing assumes omnivore defaults; vegan brands have few concentrated channels.
  • Globally distributed. Place data spans 70 countries and ~500 cities; user distribution is currently unknown and almost certainly far narrower.

03 - For business owners

If you run a vegan or vegan-friendly venue

Your place may already be on PlantsPack. We import from open data sources (OpenStreetMap, VegGuide, Foursquare) and then manually verify a subset - currently the ~10,900 venues tagged fully vegan. The remaining listings carry source-inherited labels that we have not independently checked. Listings are free; claiming is free.

What you get, free

  • A discoverable profile with map, hours, photos, tags and reviews
  • The "fully vegan" verified badge - applied after we manually check the menu and run a verification search
  • Inclusion in city pages that we keep SEO-indexable
  • Honest labelling: vegan-friendly venues are tagged as such, not promoted as fully vegan

What you can do today

  • Email us to request edits to hours, photos, description, vegan level
  • Flag a wrong listing for removal or correction
  • Share your PlantsPack link with customers

Not yet shipped: self-serve venue claiming, owner dashboard, owner replies to reviews. These are on the roadmap; until then, edits go through us by email and are typically actioned within a few days.

Why this is worth 5 minutes

A correct, fully-vegan-labelled listing on a directory that does not mix vegan and non-vegan results is more useful to a vegan customer than the same listing on a generic map. We make no claim about absolute conversion, traffic, or revenue uplift - we are too early to promise numbers we cannot back up.

04 - For marketers, agencies & creators

An aligned channel - small today, niche-pure

If your brand is vegan, plant-based, cruelty-free, or sustainable lifestyle, PlantsPack is an aligned channel. Today the audience is small (tens of users, modest organic traffic), so we will not pretend to offer reach. What we offer is alignment: every place we feature in editorial content is vegan or clearly labelled vegan-friendly, and our content standards are stricter than mixed directories.
